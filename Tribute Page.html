About Stephen Hawking

Stephen William Hawking was an English physicist born on January 8, 1942. He is considered the greatest theoretical physicist of all time. He revolutionized physics with his work on the origin of the universe and the theory of black hole explosions. From the Big Bang to Black Holes, all of his best-selling books have captivated physics enthusiasts around the world. British theoretical physicist whose theory of black hole explosions is based on the theory of relativity and quantum mechanics. He also worked in the space-time singularity field

Early Life and Education

Stephen Hawking was born on January 8, 1942 in Oxford, England. His father was Frank Hawking, an English biologist. His mother was Isabel Walker, a Scottish graduate of Philosophy, Political Science and Economics. Both his parents were graduates of Oxford University. Stephen had his two younger sisters and his one adopted son. Stephen Hawking was an average student at school who was very interested in science. After winning a science scholarship at age 17, he graduated from University College, Oxford with a first class degree in physics at age 20. Hawking then completed his doctorate in astronomy and cosmology from Trinity Hall, Cambridge University. Dr. Hawking was diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) at the age of 21, shortly after entering Cambridge University. ALS is a motor neuron disease in which nerves that control muscles become inactive while sensory nerves function normally. Initially, his doctors expected him to die within two years of his life. It took him about 40 hours to give his 45 minute lecture because of this condition.
